Sea-Bites

Sea-Bites was a project I got to work on during my UX elective at UTS. In collaboration with M&C Saatchi and Minderoo we developed a concept to solve a problem statement and promote ocean conservation by 2026. Our deliverable was a concept pitch showcasing our idea for endangered animal shaped sushi lunchbox kits to raise ocean awareness from kids at a young age.

Using the double diamond technique, we conducted extensive research, including interviews, POV statements, HMW questions, surveys, persona creation, and empathy mapping. Our goal was to develop a solution that would encourage users to "Understand, love and act for a flourishing ocean". After a thorough analysis of our research findings, we identified an effective solution: creating a sushi kit for primary school children that would introduce more sustainable seafood into their diets in a fun and engaging way.

Understanding the Problem
Designing Ocean Engagement

I particularly enjoyed this process of designing an approach that led to a more refined and informed design process, in which we were able to start to shape our project based of our personas that we created based of interviews we undertook on real target audiences from the public. Our concept to connect children to the ocean through seafood developed after user research to reveal seafood is absent in a lot of young children's diets. Sea-Bites aims to instil the love for seafood and the ocean by educating them at a young age.

Concept Development

The Seabites concept utilizes kits available at Woolworths to create endangered sea creatures, teaching kids about the importance of the ocean while promoting healthy eating habits. We were thrilled to be able to use our design skills to make a positive impact on the environment and inspire future generations to care for the ocean.

Reflection

This project allowed me to experience the UX design Process in full depth which has impacted the way I see UX design now on a daily basis. I always thought the solution to every problem was digital but  after our extensive research in this project it was apparent that that is not always the case. The experience start to finish was very helpful for my design practice and it was a fun project to work on.
