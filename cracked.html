Cracked Magazine

In this project, the objective was to design a magazine masthead, manipulate images and type within a grid system, and create an interactive digital space with a motion animated cover. It provided the opportunity to explore personal style, experiment with new mediums, and develop understanding in grid systems, print production, motion design, and typographic detailing.

This was an exciting project that allowed me the creative freedom to explore my own style through colours, imagery and grid systems through experimenting with new mediums in the print space and the AR cover. Throughout the project, I evolved my understanding of grid systems, print production, motion design, and typographic detailing influenced heavily by work from ray gun magazine and monster children . I enjoyed the process of experimenting with various design elements, such as typography, colour, and imagery, to create an engaging and visually appealing magazine cover and I feel like as the project went on I became more passionate about the work and started to develop my own design identity with the work, making me realise what kind of style appeals to me as a designer.

Exploring AR

One of the highlights of this project was creating an augmented motion graphic cover view that appeared when viewing the cover through the camera of a phone. This added an interactive element to the design, making it even more engaging for the audience, while also adding more depth to the style I was going for. It was super fun to bring the cover into my familiar space of After Effects, And I think the pixelated glitch effect introduces the audience to the style of the magazine in a intriguing way. Overall, this project allowed me to further develop my skills in motion design and typography, and I am excited to work on more projects like this in the future.
